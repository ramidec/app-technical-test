Strategic Pillar 1
01
Proof of Concept / Shipped

Intelligence

AI-Powered Message Formatter

The Problem

Users frequently send poorly formatted, unclear, or tonally mismatched messages in professional contexts. Miscommunication costs teams hours in clarification threads, and many users hesitate to send at all because they're unsure how their message will land.

The Solution

A one-tap AI formatter powered by Google Gemini 2.5 Flash. Users type a rough draft, press the sparkle icon, optionally set a tone (professional, friendly, apologetic), and receive a polished, Slack-appropriate message. The original draft is preserved for comparison.

What was built

AISheet bottom sheet modal Gemini API integration Animated gradient sparkle icon Tone/intent selector Error handling with retry Debug dashboard status Connection health check System prompt engineering
Technical Stack
Google Gemini 2.5 Flash @gorhom/bottom-sheet MaskedView + LinearGradient Reanimated shimmer expo-haptics
Business Impact

Reduces miscommunication by helping users express intent clearly. In a team context, this saves time on follow-up clarifications. The AI button also serves as a differentiation feature: it positions the app beyond commodity chat toward intelligent communication tools.

Proposed

Connectivity

Huddles: Live Audio Rooms

The Problem

There's a big gap between async text and scheduling a full video call. A long message thread that spirals for 30 minutes could be resolved in a quick voice conversation. But joining a "meeting" feels heavy: you need a link, a calendar invite, a camera check. Teams need something lighter.

The Solution

Lightweight, tap-to-start audio rooms within any conversation. No scheduling, no links, no friction. Think Slack Huddles: one tap starts a room, others see a banner and join with one more tap. The "hallway conversation" that remote teams have been missing since 2020.

Proposed features

One-tap huddle start Presence indicators Floating mini-player Invite to huddle Screen sharing Online / Away / Offline status Mute/unmute with haptics Active speaker indicator

Why this matters in 2026

Remote and hybrid work is the permanent norm. The companies that shaped modern messaging (Slack, Teams, Discord) all converged on the same insight: text + voice in the same interface beats switching between separate tools. Teams need spontaneous synchronous communication, and the gap between "text thread" and "scheduled call" is where productivity drops.

Technical Considerations

This needs research into real-time audio options for React Native. There are several approaches worth evaluating, from lower-level WebRTC bindings to managed SDKs that handle the infrastructure. The key questions are around iOS audio session handling (background playback, system integration) and keeping the experience fully native. The existing Reanimated and gesture handler setup would carry over well for the mini-player and controls.

Business Impact

Voice features naturally increase engagement because they give users a reason to come back beyond reading text. Users who can talk in real time within the app are more likely to stay active daily. This is also the feature that most clearly moves the product from a messaging tool into a communication platform.

Strategic Pillar 3
03
Proposed

Knowledge

Shared Canvases: Collaborative Workspace

The Problem

Chat is inherently ephemeral. Decisions, links, specifications, and context get buried under new messages within hours. Teams compensate by juggling multiple tools (Notion, Docs, Confluence, Sheets, Figma), but the source of truth fragments and onboarding new team members becomes an archaeology exercise.

The Solution

A persistent, editable canvas pinned to each conversation. Similar to Slack Canvases or Notion pages, but embedded in the chat. Teams can collaboratively build meeting notes, project briefs, checklists, and decision logs right where the conversation happens. The canvas persists, versions, and stays accessible even as chat scrolls on.

Proposed features

Rich text editing Checklists & task tracking Pinned resources & links @mentions with notifications Version history Real-time multi-user editing Canvas templates Inline images & code blocks

Why this matters in 2026

Tool consolidation is one of the defining trends. Teams don't want more apps; they want fewer, better-connected ones. The chat app that also handles persistent knowledge wins the workspace. Notion, Confluence, and Google Docs all live outside the conversation context. A canvas that lives inside chat eliminates the context switch and keeps decisions tied to the discussions that produced them.

Technical Considerations

The main challenge is building a rich text editor that feels fully native on mobile. There are emerging React Native solutions for structured content editing. The offline-first approach would build on the existing MMKV persistence layer. This needs deeper research to find the right balance between editing capabilities and a native-quality experience.

Business Impact

Canvases create sticky content: every checklist, every meeting note, every pinned decision is a reason for the team to come back. This drives retention through accumulated value. It also positions the app for expansion from "chat tool" to "team OS," which opens up enterprise use cases. Teams that store knowledge in your platform are far less likely to switch away.

Feasibility First

The AI Formatter is already shipped as a working proof of concept. It demonstrates technical execution and validates the "intelligence layer" thesis before investing in larger bets. Low risk, immediate value.

Retention Driver

Huddles target daily active engagement. Voice features create habits that text alone cannot: users come back not just to read, but to connect. This is the highest-impact bet in the portfolio.

Moat Builder

Canvases create switching costs through accumulated content. Every checklist, every meeting note, every decision log makes the team more invested in the platform. This is how you build a product teams can't leave.

What I considered but deprioritized

Message Reactions

High UX value but incremental. Doesn't open new use cases. Better as a polish item than a strategic pillar.

Threaded Replies

Important for scale but adds complexity without changing the product category. Canvases solve the deeper problem (persistent context).

End-to-End Encryption

Critical for production but a security feature, not a product differentiator. Infrastructure work that doesn't directly drive engagement metrics.

Video Calls

Audio-first is the right MVP. Video adds a lot of complexity (camera, bandwidth, UI) for diminishing returns on the initial use case.
